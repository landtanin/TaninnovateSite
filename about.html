About

Developer turned founder

I started as a software developer, but quickly realised the most interesting problems weren't in code — they were in the industries that hadn't been touched by good software yet. Demolition sites running on clipboards. Advisory firms with outdated web presences. Toastmasters clubs managing meetings over email chains. That gap between what technology could do and what people were actually using became my focus.

Where it started

My first client was Salter Demolition. Their team recorded material audits by hand — notes in a spreadsheet, photos on a phone, matched up later. I built Material Audit: an iPad app where you upload your floor plan, capture materials and photos on the spot, and generate the full report in one tap. It cut their auditing time by more than 5x.

I also built the website for Blackheath Advisors — a board advisory practice run by an experienced governance consultant working with Chairs and CEOs. The brief was to create something that reflects the calibre of her work and gives prospective clients confidence from the first visit.

I specialise in iOS and iPad development — building native apps that feel right in your hands and work where you need them, not just where you have WiFi.

Beyond code

I'm an active member of Toastmasters, where I've developed my public speaking and leadership skills. That community showed me another gap worth filling, which led me to co-found ToastHost — a platform that makes running a Toastmasters club less admin and more impact.

I'm based in the UK, working independently under Taninnovate. I keep things lean, move quickly, and care deeply about shipping products that real people actually use.
